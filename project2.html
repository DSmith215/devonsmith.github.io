(ii) Data

This data comes from the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS), which is a government program that completes telephone surveys across all 50 US States and territories, collecting data about people’s health and well being. The dataset currently only has data from 2011 through 2023.

This dataset contains over 20 columns and 104272 rows. It is an extremely valuable dataset often used by scientists and researchers to examine health/fitness in the United States. It contains variables including questions asked, percentage values, sex, states, coordinates, education, age, and year collected.

Most of my analysis is completed on the variable ‘Data_Value’. This variable is referenced by the variable ‘Question’, which gives context of it’s meaning. For example, for the question “percent of adults aged 18 years and older who have obesity”, ‘Data Value’ gives the metric to measure that question, between 1 and 100 percent.

Data Cleaning

Data Cleaning is performed to make the data ‘tidy’ for analysis. Most real data is often extremely messy to perform analysis on, so I completed the following steps to make this dataset more efficent for analysis:

-Lowercase everything

-Separate Both Age and Income to Upper / Lower Bounds (and remove symbols)

-Address Null Values (explained below)

When deciding what to do with the missing values, I won’t be able to delete the rows with missing variables as the entire dataset will be left with no observations, as every row is missing at least one observation.

Therefore, I have decided to replace all instances of NA’s with the median value for that column. I chose the median over the mean due to the distribution visualizations below, as well as the summary statistics for median/mean below, as some of the distributions are heavily skewed. Choosing the median allows a value that is not skewed by outliers in the data.

(iv) Analysis 1

Analysis 1: Checking the Relationship between Obesity and Time (2011-2023) using a bar plot.

Based on the data, it seems obesity has steadily increased over the past 12 years in the United States. In 2011, the percentage of US Adults with obesity was a little over 40%, which has risen to over 50% most recently in 2023.

While most of the data regarding the age of the respondents of this dataset is null, it is important to consider that this dataset may mostly contain people of a certain age group. If the BRFSS mainly went out to members of an older age group, than this dataset would reflect the older population in the US.

(v) Analysis 2

Analysis 2: Checking the Relationship between Physical Activity and Time (2011-2023) using a bar plot.

Based on the data, it seems that physical activity across all 50 states has increased over the past 12 years. There is a gap during the years on 2020 and 2021, which could potentially be due to the covid-19 pandemic. Physical activity spiked in the most recent year published as well, which could be due to other cofounding variables.

I personally found this result especially interesting, as obesity / physical activity have a inverse relationship in the current existing academic literature.

The purpose of this analysis was to investigate the different relationships between Nutrition, Obesity, Fitness, and Time in the United States, to assess our progress as a country towards healthier lifestyles.

This analysis highlighted that Obesity Rates seem to be rising in the United States while Physical Activity Rates seem to be increasing as well.
